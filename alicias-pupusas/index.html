Our Story

A Family Dream.
A Salvadoran Tradition.

Alicia's Pupusas International Restaurant is the fulfillment of owner Alicia Tacuri's lifelong dream. Tucked away on Cottonwood Street in North Platte, this family-run kitchen has earned a devoted following for one reason: the food is real.

Every pupusa is hand-pressed to order using time-honored Salvadoran technique. The corn masa is fresh, the fillings are generous, and the curtido — the light, tangy cabbage slaw served alongside — is made in-house. Customers walk in as strangers and leave feeling like family.

What Is a Pupusa?
A thick, hand-pressed corn tortilla from El Salvador — stuffed with fillings, then grilled until the outside is perfectly crisp and the inside melts. Served with curtido and house salsa.
🌸
Loroco — The Flavor You've Never Tried
Loroco is an edible flower bud native to Central America with a delicate, earthy flavor. Alicia's uses it as a traditional filling — a taste most people in North Platte have never encountered.
🥗
Curtido & House Salsas
Every order comes with handmade curtido — a lightly fermented cabbage slaw — and house-made salsas. The complete Salvadoran experience, made daily.
