Our Story

Five Generations
of Alabama BBQ

In 1944, the Hatcher family began smoking pork in a hand-dug pit in their backyard, using live oak and pecan coals. Two years later, that backyard tradition became a restaurant. Today, Lannie's Bar-B-Q Spot is one of the oldest and most celebrated barbecue joints in Alabama.

Now in its fifth generation of family ownership, nothing about the cooking has changed. The pits are still fired the same way, the sauce still carries that signature vinegar zing, and every plate still tells the story of a family that gave Selma something it will never forget.

A Living Legacy

More Than a
Restaurant

Lannie's has been part of Selma's story through its most defining moments. It was one of the city's first racially integrated dining spaces, and it stood at the center of the Civil Rights Movement that changed America.

1944
The Hatcher family begins smoking pork in a hand-dug backyard pit over live oak and pecan coals.
1946
Lannie's officially opens as a restaurant, founded by Lannie Moore Travis.
1965
Voting-rights activists gather at Lannie's to celebrate news of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.'s arrival in Selma.
2024
After tornado damage, Lannie's reopens in a new building with a 150-person dining room — five times the original size.
2025
Named one of only 44 restaurants nationwide on USA Today's Restaurants of the Year list.
